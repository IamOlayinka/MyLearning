History

Ondo State was created on February 3, 1976, during the regime of General Murtala Mohammed, by splitting the former Western State into several smaller states. Originally, Ondo State was part of the Western Region of Nigeria.

Culture

Ethnic Groups: The majority ethnic group in Ondo State is the Yoruba. However, there are also smaller ethnic groups like the Ikale, Ijaw, and Owo, which contribute to the state’s diversity.

Language: The main language spoken in Ondo State is Yoruba. However, there are also dialects such as Owo, Ikale, and Ijaw depending on the region.

Festivals: Ondo State is home to many traditional festivals, such as the Odua Festival and the Igogo Festival. These festivals celebrate the rich cultural heritage of the Yoruba people, featuring music, dance, and colorful displays.

Landmarks

Idanre Hills: One of the most famous tourist attractions in Ondo State is Idanre Hills, located near Idanre Town. The hills are known for their scenic beauty, historic significance, and the ancient settlement of the Idanre people. The site is recognized for its breathtaking views, climbing paths, and the historic Owa’s Palace. Owo Museum of Traditional

Arts: Located in Owo, this museum is a showcase of the rich cultural and traditional heritage of the people of Ondo State.

Alaseja Beach: Ondo's coastline offers some beautiful beaches such as Alaseja Beach, where visitors can enjoy the tranquility of the sea.

Ondo State Cultural Centre: Located in Akure, this center highlights the state's vibrant arts, music, and culture.

Economy

Agriculture: Agriculture is a significant part of Ondo State’s economy. The state is one of the largest producers of cocoa, palm oil, and cassava in Nigeria. Ondo is also known for its vast oil palm plantations and rubber farming.

Oil and Natural Resources: Ondo State has been increasingly involved in the oil industry. The state contains significant oil reserves, especially in the coastal areas. This has brought some level of economic diversification beyond agriculture.

Forestry and Mining: Ondo is rich in forest resources, and its mining activities focus on minerals like limestone, granite, and clay.
